The
Chronicle

The whole human story — from the first city in the mud of Sumer to the world we live in now. Told slowly, and in full.
How to read this: every load-bearing claim carries a numbered citation to its source. Claims are graded attested (documented in sources or archaeology), inferred (scholarly consensus from indirect evidence), or legend (tradition, told as tradition). Where scholars disagree, the disagreement is stated. Dates use BCE/CE — the same years as the older BC/AD. Chapters are numbered by era and position — II·3 is the third chapter of the Classical World. A civilization is shelved in the era where its story begins. The Synchronic View

The World,
Year by Year

History books tell one story at a time. But the world never happened one story at a time. Freeze a year, and look around the whole planet.Fifteen moments · 3000 BCE – 1900 CE

c. 3000 BCE

The first cities · writing is brand new
Mesopotamia

Uruk is the largest settlement on earth; cuneiform writing is spreading through the temple storehouses.

Egypt

The Nile kingdoms have just been unified under the first pharaohs; hieroglyphs appearing.

Indus

Farming towns of the early Harappan era — the great cities are still centuries away.

Europe

Megalithic tomb-builders; Stonehenge exists only as an earthwork circle. Ötzi the Iceman died in the Alps about three centuries earlier.

China

Neolithic farming villages along the Yellow River; no writing, no dynasties yet.

Americas

Maize farming spreading in Mexico; on the Peruvian coast, the first stirrings of what will become Caral.

c. 2500 BCE

The pyramid age
Egypt

The Great Pyramid of Giza has just been completed — the tallest building on earth for the next 3,800 years.

Mesopotamia

The Sumerian city-states are at war; the death-pit burials of Ur’s royal cemetery belong to this era.

Uruk

A generation or two earlier or later, roughly here, sits the reign later poets would remember as Gilgamesh, king of Uruk — within a century or two of Kish's archaeologically attested king Enmebaragesi.

Indus

Mohenjo-daro and Harappa near their height — the largest planned cities in the world.

Anatolia

The citadel of Troy (Troy II) flourishes, rich in gold.

Britain

The great sarsen stones of Stonehenge are being raised.

Peru

Caral — the first city of the Americas — is flourishing, with monumental platforms.

c. 2300 BCE

The first empire
Mesopotamia

Sargon’s Akkadian Empire — the first empire in history — rules from the Gulf toward the Mediterranean.

Assyria

Ashur is a modest town under Akkadian sway; its merchant centuries are still ahead.

Egypt

The Old Kingdom is past its pyramid-building peak and drifting toward crisis.

Indus

The mature cities trade with Mesopotamia, where their land is called Meluhha.

Europe

Bell Beaker cultures spread metalworking across the continent.

c. 2000 BCE

After the fall of Ur
Mesopotamia

Ur has just fallen to Elam (2004 BCE); Amorite kingdoms are carving up the land — Babylon is still a village.

Assyria

The Kanesh merchant caravans are about to make Ashur rich — the opening act of Assyria’s story.

Egypt

The Middle Kingdom has reunified Egypt after a century of division.

Indus

The great cities are beginning their long, mysterious decline.

Crete

The first Minoan palaces rise — Europe’s first civilization.

China

Late Longshan culture; the era of the legendary Xia dynasty; bronze-working Erlitou soon after.

c. 1750 BCE

The age of Hammurabi
Babylon

Hammurabi has united Mesopotamia and carved his law in stone; the Ea-nasir copper complaints are being filed at Ur.

Egypt

The Middle Kingdom is waning; Asiatic settlers in the Delta foreshadow the Hyksos era.

Anatolia

The Old Hittite kingdom is taking shape.

Crete

Minoan palace civilization at its height — Knossos, frescoes, the bull-leapers.

India

The Indus cities are largely abandoned; village cultures carry on.

China

Erlitou casts the first Chinese bronzes.

c. 1450 BCE

The imperial Bronze Age
Egypt

The New Kingdom empire is at its greatest extent under Thutmose III, from Syria to Nubia.

Anatolia

The Hittites are becoming a great power.

Babylon

The Kassite kings preside over a stable, quiet Babylonia.

Assyria

Still in the shadow of Mitanni — its imperial turn is a century away.

Greece

Mycenaean warlords take over Minoan Knossos; the palace age of mainland Greece.

China

The Shang dynasty rules from the Yellow River; oracle-bone writing appears in the following centuries.

c. 1200 BCE

The Bronze Age Collapse
E. Mediterranean

The interconnected Bronze Age world is falling like dominoes — the Hittite empire vanishes, Ugarit burns, the Sea Peoples are on the move.

Egypt

Ramesses III repels the Sea Peoples — Egypt survives, but its imperial age is ending.

Mesopotamia

Assyria and Babylon weather the storm, weakened.

Greece

The Mycenaean palaces burn; Greece enters a dark age. The legend of the Trojan War is set in this world.

China

Late Shang — the oracle bones of Anyang record the kings’ questions to their ancestors.

Americas

The Olmec centre of San Lorenzo flourishes in Mexico, its colossal stone heads already carved.

c. 900 BCE

The Iron Age dawn
Assyria

The Neo-Assyrian rise begins — the war machine the Bible remembers.

Levant

The kingdoms of Israel and Judah take shape.

Egypt

Divided, ruled in part by Libyan pharaohs of the Twenty-second Dynasty — the long twilight has begun.

Nubia

Napata is re-emerging as an organized Egyptianized kingdom under King Alara — within two centuries its kings will rule Egypt itself.

Greece

Recovering from the dark age; within a century it will borrow the Phoenician alphabet.

India

Vedic kingdoms and the first great cities of the Ganges plain.

China

The Western Zhou rule under the Mandate of Heaven.

c. 550 BCE

The Axial Age
Persia

Cyrus is assembling the largest empire the world has seen.

Babylon

Nabonidus, Babylon’s strange last king, reigns — the gates open to Cyrus in 539.

Nubia

Kush has withdrawn to Napata, then Meroe, after a century ruling Egypt as its 25th Dynasty — sacked once by Assyria's allies, once by Egypt itself, and still standing.

Greece

The city-states flourish; Athens is a generation from inventing democracy.

India

The age of the Buddha; the Ganges kingdoms compete.

China

The age of Confucius; the Zhou order is fragmenting.

Rome

A small city ruled by its last kings.

c. 330 BCE

The world Alexander broke open
Persia

Persepolis burns; the Achaemenid Empire falls to Alexander.

Greece & Macedon

Alexander of Macedon, having conquered Greece, now overruns the Persian Empire; the age of the free city-state is over.

Egypt

Alexander is welcomed as liberator; the Ptolemaic age — ending with Cleopatra — begins.

India

The Nanda empire rules the Ganges; Chandragupta Maurya is about to overthrow it.

China

The Warring States forge the ideas and armies that Qin will soon unify.

Rome

The Republic is conquering Italy, unnoticed by the great powers of the east.

c. 1 CE

Two empires bookend the earth
Rome

Augustus has turned the Republic into an empire; the Mediterranean is a Roman lake.

Persia

The Parthians rule Iran and Mesopotamia — Rome’s stubborn eastern rival.

India

Sangam-era Tamil kingdoms trade with Rome; the north between empires.

China

Han China — Rome’s equal in size and wealth; silk moves west along the new roads.

Egypt

A Roman province now; Cleopatra died three decades ago.

Americas

Teotihuacan rises in Mexico; the Maya lowland cities are growing.

c. 500 CE

After Rome
Byzantium

The Western empire has fallen (476); Constantinople carries Rome on.

Persia

The Sasanians at their height — Byzantium’s great rival.

India

The Gupta golden age is ending under Huna invasions.

China

Divided north and south; reunification is a century away.

Americas

The Classic Maya flourish; Teotihuacan dominates Mexico.

Europe

Frankish and Gothic kingdoms rise on Roman ruins.

c. 1000 CE

The medieval world at full sail
China

Song China is the richest, most inventive society on earth.

Islamic world

From Córdoba to Baghdad — the golden age of Islamic science.

Byzantium

At its medieval peak under Basil II.

India

The Cholas rule the south and send fleets across the Bay of Bengal.

The north

Vikings touch Newfoundland; Kievan Rus adopts Christianity.

Americas

Cahokia rises on the Mississippi; the Toltecs dominate Mexico.

c. 1450 CE

On the eve of the modern
Ottomans

About to take Constantinople (1453) and end Byzantium.

China

Ming China has ended its treasure voyages and turned inward.

India

Vijayanagara at its height in the south; the Delhi Sultanate fading.

Americas

The Aztec alliance and the expanding Inca — both near their peaks, unaware of what is coming.

Europe

The Renaissance stirs in Italy; printing is being invented in Mainz.

Africa

Songhai rises on the Niger; Great Zimbabwe wanes.

c. 1900 CE

The world the Chronicle ends in
Britain

The empire covers a quarter of the earth; the Raj rules India.

China

The Qing dynasty is in its final decade.

Ottomans

The “sick man of Europe” nears its end.

Japan

Meiji Japan has industrialized in a single generation.

The rising

The United States and Germany — the new industrial giants.

Africa

Partitioned among European powers within a single generation.
